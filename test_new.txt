Prompt Engineering — Overview
What is Prompt Engineering?
It’s simply how you talk to AI to get useful results.
Think of it like briefing a colleague:
• Tell AI what you need
• Share relevant context
• Specify how you want the output
• Give examples when helpful
In short:
👉 Better instructions = Better answers.
You’re not changing the AI model —
you’re improving how you ask the question.
Why Does It Matter for Your Work?
Good prompting helps you:
✅ Get clearer, more relevant answers
(less rework, fewer follow-ups)
✅ Save time on analysis, drafting & summaries
✅ Control output format
(slides, tables, emails, bullet points)
✅ Improve consistency across tasks
✅ Reduce risk by guiding tone and content
Optional closing line (very effective):
Prompt engineering turns AI from a “chatbot” into a practical work assistant.

Prompt Engineering: The New Core Skill for Working with AI
A prompt is simply how you brief AI — your instruction, question, and context.
❌ Vague Prompts (Hard for AI to Help)
Write a fund commentary
Summarize this PDF
Explain portfolio performance
(These sound familiar — but they don’t tell AI what matters.)
✅ Better Prompts (Clear Business Context)
Client Business Example
Write a Q1 2024 fund commentary for a UK equity fund, highlighting sector rotation, top contributors/detractors, and positioning versus benchmark. Keep it client-friendly and under 300 words.
DAO / Analytics Example
Summarize key ESG risks and macro themes from this 10-page ESG Strategy document. Output as bullet points with a short executive summary.
Portfolio Analytics Example
Explain Q3 underperformance of our multi-asset fund, focusing on interest rate impact and asset allocation decisions. Format as 3 key drivers with supporting data.
Simple Prompting Tips
✅ Be clear about your goal
✅ Provide context (fund, region, period, theme)
✅ Specify output format (bullets, table, summary)
✅ Choose role or style (client-ready, analyst tone)
✅ Add constraints (word count, focus areas)
Closing line (optional, powerful):
Think of AI like a junior analyst — the better your brief, the better the result.

Prompt Engineering Techniques
1. X-Shot Prompting (Zero / One / Few Examples)
Structure
Provide examples in your prompt to show AI what good looks like.
Zero-shot → no example
One-shot → one example
Few-shot → multiple examples
Example (DAO / Client Business)
One-shot example:
You are a client reporting analyst.
Example output:
“Q2 performance was driven by overweight Industrials and underweight Technology.”
Now summarize Q3 performance of our Global Equity Fund in the same style.
How it works
✅ Examples guide output style and structure
✅ Reduces ambiguity
✅ Improves consistency across reports
✅ Especially useful for ESG tagging, commentary templates, and analytics summaries
2. Role Prompting (Persona-based prompting)
Structure
“You are a [ROLE].
Your task is to [TASK].
Use this context: [DATA].
Output in [FORMAT].”
Example (HSBC AM)
You are a fund analyst.
Your task is to summarize Q2 performance of our Asia Equity Fund.
Use this context: China weakness, strong India allocation, USD strength.
Provide 3 bullet points followed by a short client-friendly paragraph.
How it works
✅ Role sets expertise level
✅ Controls tone (client-ready vs analyst)
✅ Improves domain relevance
✅ Makes outputs feel professionally written
3. Chain-of-Thought (Step-by-Step Decomposition)
(Best for analytics & complex reasoning)
Structure
Ask AI to break the problem into steps before answering:
“First… Next… Then… Finally…”
Example (DAO / Portfolio Analytics)
The multi-asset portfolio underperformed in Q3.
First, identify macro drivers.
Next, analyze asset allocation impact.
Then, summarize sector effects.
Finally, provide a short executive summary.
How it works
✅ Encourages structured reasoning
✅ Reduces hallucination
✅ Mimics how analysts think
✅ Helps with performance attribution, ESG analysis, and multi-factor explanations
Optional Closing Line for Slide
These techniques turn AI from a chatbot into a junior analyst that follows your instructions.

Prompt Engineering | COSTAR Framework
A Simple Checklist for Better Prompts
What is COSTAR?
Think of COSTAR as your pre-flight checklist before sending a prompt.
If you cover these 6 elements, AI gives clearer, more relevant, more usable outputs.
COSTAR =
C — Context
What background does AI need?
(Fund, region, time period, data source)
O — Objective
What exactly do you want done?
(Summarize, explain, compare, analyze)
S — Style
How should it be written?
(Client-friendly, analyst-level, technical)
T — Tone
What’s the voice?
(Professional, concise, neutral)
A — Audience
Who is this for?
(Client, PM, internal stakeholders)
R — Response Format
How should it be delivered?
(Bullets, table, slide-ready text)
