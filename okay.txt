Over the past 12 months, Nadeeha has been a critical contributor to HSBC Asset Management's push to bring transformative AI and data science capabilities into investment research and sustainable finance. Based in India as Associate Data Science Lead within the Investment Value Stream, she works in close partnership with the Innovation & Quant Lab (IQ Lab) under the Chief Investment Office, along with sustainability, compliance, and operations teams across the UK, to deliver solutions that are reshaping how the firm builds and manages thematic investment products. Her most significant contribution has been to the QuantThematics project, where she developed the data science and GenAI components that power the firm's proprietary thematic investment signals. Her work is enabling HSBC AM to build 8+ internal thematic universes to replace reliance on third-party ETFs in the Multi-Asset Multi-Thematic Fund, with a longer-term contribution to Discretionary Portfolio Management targeting $100bn+ in AUM by 2029. The estimated commercial impact is $8.4M to $16.4M, with significant savings in client fees ($2.7M), FTE costs ($100k annually), and data license fees. Beyond QuantThematics, she delivered high quality analytics for HSBC AM's proprietary ESG Score framework, work that was presented at a strategic offsite in France and used by senior decision makers to refine the scoring methodology. She built the Policy Dashboard and ESMA Dashboard for the Sustainability Office, giving stakeholders granular visibility into fund exposures across SFDR classifications and sensitive ESG themes like Oil & Gas, Tobacco, and Thermal Coal, and these dashboards now play a central role in risk assessment, regulatory compliance, and investment oversight. Her automation of Financed Carbon Emissions calculations under the Net Zero Asset Management initiative supports HSBC AM's commitment to reducing financed emissions by 58% across its portfolios by 2030. She has also delivered strong proof-of-concept work including an EDD Scoring automation using GenAI to extract sector-specific ESG disclosures from sustainability reports, and a Thematic Scoring model for a Social Opportunity Fund, both of which demonstrated clear FTE savings and built internal confidence in the team's AI capabilities.
Nadeeha has also made a meaningful contribution to building AI capability and data literacy well beyond her own team. She has trained over 150 colleagues across Asset Management and Wealth & Premier Banking through sessions on prompt engineering, AI fundamentals, and data science concepts. Within Asset Management specifically, these sessions have helped investment professionals understand how GenAI and data-driven tools can be applied to their workflows, from portfolio analysis to ESG research, raising the overall technical fluency of the teams she works alongside. She was also selected as a captain in HSBC's group-wide Coaching Circle initiative, where she facilitated peer support sessions focused on mental wellbeing and personal development across business lines. On the talent side, she has been hands-on in growing the next generation of investment technology professionals, mentoring interns in the Data and Analytics Office's data science team through the full journey from onboarding to building real domain expertise in asset management data science. Both have since converted into full-time data scientist roles, a tangible example of her commitment to developing diverse talent for the investment industry. Her team at the NOVA Hackathon, where she co-developed a GenAI-powered TCFD Reporting Tool for automating climate-related financial disclosures, was selected among just 27 finalist teams from across the entire HSBC Group, further showing her ability to innovate and collaborate at scale.
What sets Nadeeha apart is that she is not just adopting AI, she is actively shaping how an entire asset management business thinks about and uses it. While the investment industry is still figuring out how to meaningfully integrate GenAI, she has been the one building the actual solutions, from thematic investment signals to automated emissions reporting to ESG document analysis, and then teaching 150+ colleagues how to use these tools in their own work. She placed 5th globally in the coding category at the Global Prompt Engineering Championship in Dubai, the only Indian representative in her category among 3,400 entries, and brought that expertise straight back into HSBC through hands-on training for investment professionals, operations teams, and wealth bankers. Having grown from a data science consultant at a Big Four firm into her current role at HSBC Asset Management, her trajectory is a compelling example of what women can achieve in technology-led investment careers. She is not waiting for the industry to catch up with AI. She is pulling it forward, and building a culture of curiosity and continuous learning around her as she does it.
